A vector as general datatype is a part of the first STL implementation. 
I think it was in 1994. It was part of early Borland C++ Compilers in 1995 / 1996 also. 
Its official part of the 1st ISO C++ Standard since 1998. 
This article show "deep" knowledge of C++ (irony off), vectors are not modern, they are part of classic C++. 
And they don't resize with each new element, implementations are different but reallocations should only 
happen at logarithmically growing intervals of size. 

The Borland C++ Compiler in 1996 had a really good Book which discribed the STL and with this vectors. 
Its important to understand containers, iterators and algorithms. A vector is far away from a dynamic array 
in other languages, self if its defined as dynamic allocated memory with contiguous storage locations for 
their elements (mostly).
